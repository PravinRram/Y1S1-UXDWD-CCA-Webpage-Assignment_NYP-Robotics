why us

Learn about the many opportunities available for you to fulfill your PEARLS CCA points with us.

Training Sessions

By joining NYP Robotics, you'll gain hard & soft skills essential throughout your life. We make sure every member, no matter technical ability, leaves with skills that matter in the real world.

You get to decide which skills you want to gain, just attend the training sessions teaching them!

Technical Skills

Mecha­nical Design & Fabric­ation

Platform(s):
3D printing, CAD modelling, Lego Mindstorms, VeX Robotics

For Beginners: Lego Mindstorms & VeX Robotics
For Advanced: 3D printing & CAD modelling.

Learn how to create robot frames and parts.

Electr­onics & Wiring

Platform(s):
Arduino, Micro:bit, ESP32 (for IoT)

For Beginners: Micro:bit
For Intermediate: Arduino
For advanced: ESP32.

Learn circuits, motors, sensors, and microcontrollers.

Progra­mming for Robots

Platform(s):
Block-based coding for Scratch, VeX Robotics (VEXcode) & Lego Mindstorms (EV3 Software), and
Text-based coding for Micro:bit (Python), Arduino (C++) & ESP32 (C++)

For Beginners: Block-based coding
For Intermediate: Python for Micro:bit
For Advanced: C++ for Arduino & ESP32.

Learn programming to bring robots to life.

Soft Skills

Problem-Solving & Creativity

  • Learn how to apply Design Thinking to tackle real-world robotics challenges
  • Learn how to debug hardware and software under tight dealines
  • Develop creative, out-of-the-box solutions, whether for hardware or software, to meet goals

Collaboration & Project Management

  • Work in cross-functional teams made up people with various expertise, like mechanical or software, to excel holistically
  • Learn how to present your work to competition judges and peers

Practical Experience

  • Build a portfolio of completed projects, events ran, competitions participated to showcase in future job or university applications
  • Gain hands-on experience with robotics platforms and equipment recognised in the industry

Projects & Events

That's why we start projects where you can apply your expertise, and host events for members & non-members. We give plenty of opportunities to build and learn.

Leade­rship

NYP Robotics runs on the passion, dedication, & creativity of our members -- and our committee members play a key role in shaping our direction & culture.

If you want to go beyond just being a member, are very passionate about our club, and want to meaningfully dedicate more of your time & energy for us, consider becoming a committee member!

Execuitive Committee (EXCO)

The EXCO is responsible for setting the direction & goals of the club, managing operations, and ensuring the club runs smoothly.

Commitment: 4 - 5 hours per week (outside regular club hours), heavier during event & volunteering periods, and before project deadlines.

Length of role: 1 year

How to apply:
Only subcommittee members can apply. At the start of the 4th term of the year, the EXCO will send an EXCO application form through communication channels, and after a selection process, will announce the new EXCO for the next year before the end of the term.

Subcommittees

Project, Events, & Publicity

This is where most of the action happens, where each subcommittee has to complete tasks and follow timelines to ensure quality and creativity in all outputs, where it be event planning, project showcases, or content creation.

Note for all subcom members:
Every subcom member must play a role in at least 1 training session.
EXCO and all subcoms will come up with a topic to teach for each training session, and EXCO will organise a team of members from each subcom to prepare and facilitate the training session for the week.

Commitment: 2 - 3 hours per week (outside regular club hours), heavier during event & volunteering periods, and before project deadlines.

Length of role: 1 semester

How to apply:
In the middle of the 2nd term of the current semester, the EXCO will send a subcommittee application form through communication channels, and after an interview, will decide and announce the new subcommittee members for the next semester before the end of the term.

Competi­tions

We participate in a variety of robotics competitions and challenges, from national to international. These are places where you can put your skills to the test, where you'll innvoate, adapt, and problem-solve under real-world scenarios.

Interested in achieving greatness? Participate in the competitions we promote on our communication channels!

NYP Robotics members aren't required to participate in competitions.
But we do strongly encourage them to participate in at least 1 during their time with us.

How to find & join competitions?
The EXCO will always be promoting national robotics competitions in communication channels, and if you're interested, just notify the EXCO members!

What platforms are tested in the competitions?
We only promote competitions that test the skills that we teach in training sessions.

Want to know skills we teach? Click below!

Training Sessions

For Interested Participants >

Your experience will depend on the competition.

You may have to join a competition team, which the EXCO can help with forming.

Time outside of regular club hours can be used to prepare, and you'll gain invaluable hands-on experience in designing, building, programming and testing.

You'll learn to effectively collaborate with your team your tight deadlines, and represent NYP while showinf your skills to a wide audience.

You'll also be working closely with the EXCO to ensure everything is running smoothly.

Commitment will increase leading up to the competition day.

Weekly to bi-weekly meetings -- Regular practice & build sessions
Pre-competition period -- May need additional evening or weekend sessions
Competition week -- Full-day commitments
